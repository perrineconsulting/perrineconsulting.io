When Emotional Hot Spots Hijack Your Brain

By Cindi Perrine | November 22, 2025

We've known for a long time that different networks within our brain are responsible for different behaviors, experiences, and thoughts. This neurological perspective of thought and behavior is increasingly entering the coaching world under various frameworks, mainly focused on two areas within our brain: the **limbic system** and the **prefrontal cortex**.

The limbic system is roughly the middle part of your brain, below the folded layers of your cerebral cortex but above your brain stem. I studied parts of the limbic system in graduate school, measuring the electrical responses in Rabbit brains while they were learning. Our research was rudimentary compared to what is possible today, but the area continues to interest scientists. There's a lot we don't know about how the brain works in all its beautiful complexity, but in a grossly simplistic way, many would describe the limbic as critical to learning, emotions, behavior (habits), and olfaction. Your **fight, flight, freeze, and fawn response** lives here. The limbic system will become a recurring topic in future writings, but I want to focus on emotions for this post.
